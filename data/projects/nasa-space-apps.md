## The Challenge: Meteor Madness

### Core Problem

A fictional newly discovered asteroid — **"Impactor-2025"** — has been identified as a potential threat to Earth. The problem: available data is:

- Scattered across separate databases (NASA for asteroids, USGS for geology)
- Too complex for the general public to understand
- Not presented with realistic impact simulations
- Not paired with protection strategy evaluation tools

### Challenge Goal

Build an interactive web tool combining:

- **NASA data:** Asteroid information (size, speed, orbit)
- **USGS data:** Geological and environmental data (terrain, earthquake zones, tsunami zones)

---

## What the Team Built

An interactive 3D asteroid impact simulator targeting scientists, decision-makers, students, and the general public.

### Features

**Simulation:**

- Physics-based asteroid trajectory calculation
- Collision energy estimation
- Crater size estimation
- Environmental effects simulation: tsunamis, earthquakes, atmospheric changes

**Interaction:**

- Sliders and dropdown controls for simulation parameters
- Interactive maps for choosing possible impact locations
- **3D visualization of asteroid trajectories using Three.js**

**Protection Strategies:**

- Simulate asteroid deflection methods (rocket propulsion, gravity tractor)
- Compare effectiveness of different strategies
- Show how deflection timing changes the asteroid's path

**Gamification:**

- "Defend the Earth" mode — users test deflection strategies in a time challenge

**Educational:**

- Visual tooltips explaining scientific terms
- Interactive storytelling around the "Impactor-2025" scenario
- Regional impact maps
