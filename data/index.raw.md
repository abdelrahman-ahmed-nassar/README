## **Core Problem**

A newly discovered asteroid called **“Impactor-2025”** has been identified as a potential threat to Earth. The challenge is that the currently available data:

- Is scattered across separate databases (NASA for asteroids, USGS for geology)
- Is too complex for the general public
- Does not provide realistic impact simulations
- Does not help evaluate protection strategies

## **Challenge Goal**

Develop an interactive web tool that combines:

- **NASA Data:** Asteroid information (size, speed, orbit)
- **USGS Data:** Geological and environmental data (terrain, earthquakes, tsunami zones)

## **Required Features**

### **1. Simulation**

- Calculate the asteroid’s trajectory using physics laws
- Estimate collision energy and expected crater size
- Simulate environmental effects (tsunamis, earthquakes, atmospheric changes)

### **2. Interaction**

- Simple control tools (sliders, dropdown menus)
- Interactive maps for possible impact locations
- 3D visualization of asteroid trajectories

### **3. Protection Strategies**

- Simulate asteroid deflection methods (rockets, gravity-based methods)
- Compare the effectiveness of different strategies
- Show how timing changes the asteroid’s path

## **Target Audience**

- **Scientists:** Advanced technical analysis
- **Decision-makers:** Clear insights for decision-making
- **Education:** Simplified learning tools
- **General Public:** Understanding risks and solutions

## **Suggested Creative Ideas**
