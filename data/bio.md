## The Story

### Before College — A Self-Made Start

Abdelrahman's story is one of discipline and self-building from an early age.

From age 9 to 17, he worked as a poultry butcher to help support himself and his family. Simultaneously, he was a top student in Al-Azhar schools every single year, achieving first place annually.

At secondary school (year 2), he got his first PC and immediately started learning programming through the **Kalbonyan Elmarsos** internship program — learning HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, Node.js, and AWS. He won first place in their programming competition.

In secondary year 3, he paused all programming to focus entirely on his national exams. He graduated with a **99% GPA**, ranked in the **top 10 students across all of Egypt**, and was officially honored and awarded.

### Preparatory Year at University

He enrolled at Al-Azhar University and fought hard in the preparatory year to qualify for the **Computer & Systems Engineering** department (his dream department). He achieved a **3.5 GPA, ranked 2nd in his class**, and was formally honored by the college.

During that same year, he created 4 YouTube courses for his classmates on his channel ([@3of-academy](https://www.youtube.com/@3of-academy)):

- Engineering Drawing
- MATLAB
- Flowcharts
- Introduction to Computers

### First Year — Building Real Products

He entered Computer Engineering and immediately started building real, production-grade software:

- Built the official **Al-Azhar Electronics Showcase** platform (adopted by the university)
- Built a full **LMS system** for a private teacher
- Learned Docker, Nginx, VPS management, and CI/CD from scratch
- Presented his work at the **AZ-SENCS Engineering Exhibition** to the Dean, Head of Department, and University President

A recruiter at the exhibition offered him an internship at **Shift Up Solutions**.

### After the Internship — Founding Learn Shift

Post-internship, he came out with a "business mentality." He founded **Learn Shift**, converted his LMS into a startup product, hired and managed 2 developers, acquired multiple clients, and expanded the product into multiple verticals (teachers, Islamic academies, kindergarten ERP).

He also launched **Modawim (مداوم)** — a SaaS productivity app that is now live and serving thousands of users.

### Currently (2nd Year / 3rd Counting Preparatory)

He is in his second year of Computer Engineering, running Learn Shift as a side project while preparing to enter the job market at a top tech company.
